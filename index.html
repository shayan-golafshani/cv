Personal Bio

In 2018, I graduated with an interdisciplinary degree, "Program in the Environment" from the University of Michigan, in Ann Arbor with a focus in Sustainable Stewardship. After college, I taught English, as well as Environmental Science on the side, in Kamiyama-cho, Tokushima Prefecture, Japan. Here, I was faced with the problem encounterd by many environmentalists. The largest problem facing the world is not educated people not caring about the Earth. Rather, many people in the world aren't well-versed with climate-science, have access to readily digestible and easy to understand data, or readily have access to the knowledge required to make an informed change in their lifestyle to live a more sustainable life.

After I realized this, I decided to dedicate my life to learning computer science principles, in the hopes that I can help share the environmental issues we're facing today as a society with the world so that the more people are armed with knowledge to make a change. I believe that the more people know the more they will act in good faith to help save the Earth.
